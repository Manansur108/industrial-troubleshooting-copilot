Motor startup troubleshooting guide

If the motor does not start after a reset:
1. Verify E-stop chain and safety relay status.
2. Confirm overload relay is reset.
3. Check that the run permissive is present from the PLC.
4. Confirm the drive has no active fault.
5. Verify the interlock device and sensor feedback are healthy.

Common causes include safety interlock open, overload relay trip, active drive fault, or missing run command.
